Mathematical Operations
First, Python doesn't use all of the commonly known symbols for mathematics. In the list below, you will learn how to preform basic mathematical operations.

 Addition: 2+2
Subtraction: 2-2
Divison:
 Integer Division: 10//3 (Answer: 3)
Float Division: 10/3 (Answer: 3.3333333333)
When dividing in Python, there are two types, integer and float division (more on floats later). When doing integer division or //, it removes the decimal places. In this example, integer division converts 3.33333 into just 3. On the other hand, float division leaves the answer in decimal form (so 3.33333).
 Multiplication: 2*2
Exponents: 2**2
Parentheses/Grouping: (2+2)*(3-9)
 Note: In Python, there are no [] or {} for math. You just use more parentheses if
needed. [] and {} are used for other things like lists and dictionaries
